Before anything else
Two separate costs — important to understand upfront
Every project has two types of cost. They are completely separate.
Build fee

Paid to Altitude AI. Once.

What you pay us for our time and expertise — designing, building, testing, and handing over the system. Our own tools are our cost, not yours.

Infrastructure costs

Paid by you to providers. Monthly.

Every system needs somewhere to live. These go directly to the companies running the infrastructure — hosting, AI, databases. We have zero markup on these.

The analogy that always lands. You hire a mechanic. You pay the mechanic for their time and skill — that is the build fee. But your car also needs parts — oil, filters, brake pads. Those are charged at cost, direct from the supplier. You would pay them regardless of which mechanic you used. Same here. Altitude AI is the mechanic. Hosting, AI usage, and platform subscriptions are the parts. Fully transparent, no markup.

The Telegram revival funnel — top to bottom
One technical thing to be upfront about. Telegram bots cannot send private DMs to group members who have never messaged the bot first — Telegram does not allow it. The conversion message goes as a tagged reply in the group thread rather than a private DM. Same outcome, slightly more public. If we want private DMs we include a pinned message asking interested members to message the bot directly — those who do can then receive DMs.
