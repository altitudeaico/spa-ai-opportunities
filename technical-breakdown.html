Sprint 01
The AI Coach — a version of Deb, available 24/7

Right now members message Deb directly with questions about the Spartan strategy, individual stocks, and how to use the watch list. He answers the same questions dozens of times a day. This sprint builds an AI version of Deb — trained on everything he knows — that answers those questions instantly, at any hour, in his voice. Members feel like they are talking to Deb. Deb gets his time back.

30-day sprint Medium complexity
Where does the AI coach live — our recommendation
✅ We recommend: WhatsApp DMs

Members message a dedicated WhatsApp number and the AI responds. No new app for members to download, no new login to remember. It lives exactly where they already spend their time. This is the right choice for Sprint 1 because it is the fastest to adopt and the fastest to build. A web-based chat interface is a better long-term home and becomes part of the watch list app in Sprint 4 — the AI brain built here plugs straight in.

⚠ One thing to be aware of on WhatsApp group auto-approval

We have described automating the process of approving new members into the WhatsApp group when they pay. To be precise about how this works — WhatsApp does not have a simple on/off switch for this. What we build is a system where, when payment is confirmed, a message is automatically sent to the new member with a direct invite link that gets them straight in. This achieves the same outcome — instant access on payment — through a reliable, supported method rather than a workaround that could break. The member experience is seamless. The mechanics just work slightly differently to how it might sound on paper.

What we actually build
The knowledge base — the AI's brain

We take everything Deb has ever created — podcast transcripts, course material, past group messages, strategy documents, watch list logic and commentary — and load it into a system the AI can search through instantly when a member asks a question.

When someone asks "should I be buying this stock right now?" the AI searches everything Deb has ever said about that kind of situation and generates a response that reflects Deb's thinking. This is not a generic chatbot. It is specifically trained on Deb's knowledge and way of thinking.

Plain English for what this means technically: think of it like giving the AI a searchable library of everything Deb knows. When a question comes in, the AI finds the most relevant pages of that library and uses them to write the answer.

Voice and style training

Beyond the content, we train the AI to communicate the way Deb communicates. His directness, his level of detail, how he handles uncertainty, what he will and will not advise on. The responses need to sound like Deb wrote them — not like a customer service bot.

We also build strict guardrails. The AI will not give specific personal investment advice or tell someone to buy a specific amount of a stock. Anything that crosses into regulated financial advice gets redirected to Deb personally, with a summary of what the member asked so he has full context before he replies.

WhatsApp connection

A dedicated WhatsApp number is set up through an official WhatsApp Business provider. Deb announces it to the community — "for any questions about the strategy, message this number." Members message it, the AI responds within seconds.

Important note: setting this up requires applying to Meta (the company that owns WhatsApp) for business API access. This takes approximately 1-2 weeks to approve. We apply on day one of the sprint so it does not delay delivery.

Escalation to Deb

When the AI detects a question it should not answer — personal financial situations, major investment decisions, or anything requiring Deb's specific judgment — it sends Deb a notification with the full conversation and a suggested response. Deb replies and his answer goes straight back to the member.

Usage monitoring

A simple dashboard showing how many questions members are asking, which topics come up most, and what the AI is costing per month in real terms. This gives Deb visibility on what his members actually want to know and whether the AI is handling it well.

What we build
Payment to access — automated

When someone pays on WOP, that platform sends an instant signal to our system with the new member's details. Our system immediately sends them a WhatsApp message containing a direct group invite link. They tap it, they are in. No waiting, no manual approval from Deb.

Plain English for the technical bit: WOP has a feature called a webhook — it is basically a notification that fires automatically when something happens (like a payment). We catch that notification and trigger the WhatsApp message. The whole thing takes about 10-15 seconds from payment to group access.

Tiered welcome messages

A £99 standard member gets a warm, clear welcome — what SPA is, what to do first, where to find the watch list. A £300 Spartan member gets a more personal, exclusive-feeling message that reflects their premium status.

All messages are written in Deb's voice during the sprint. He reads and approves every one before they go live. Nothing sends without his sign-off.

The 7-day onboarding journey

A sequence of messages that fires automatically based on when someone joined — not a fixed calendar. Day 1 is always their first day regardless of when they paid.

Cancellation handling

When a member cancels or a payment fails, the system handles it automatically. Failed payments get a polite reminder and a 48-hour grace period before anything happens — recovering members who simply forgot to update their card. Genuine cancellations get one final message offering a pause or alternative before access is removed.

When access is removed, it happens across WhatsApp and WOP simultaneously. Nothing leaks through.

⚠ An important technical limitation to be upfront about

Telegram bots can post in groups and read when someone replies or reacts. However, a bot cannot send a private DM to a group member who has never messaged the bot directly first — Telegram does not allow it. This means the conversion message goes into the group rather than as a private DM to the individual. In practice: when someone engages with a post, the bot replies to them directly in the group thread (tagging them) with a message pointing to the paid group. This is slightly more public than a private DM but it is transparent, it works within Telegram's rules, and it still drives the conversion. If we want private DMs, we include a message at the start of the revival asking people to message the bot directly to receive exclusive updates — those who do can then receive DMs.

What we build
The Telegram posting agent

A bot deployed into the existing group that posts on a schedule. Content is generated automatically from the watch list and recent market commentary — written in Deb's tone. Each post includes a clear link to the paid WhatsApp group.

Posting schedule: daily for the first two weeks to re-establish presence, then 3-4 times per week ongoing. Deb can adjust this at any time.

Engagement detection and conversion reply

The bot monitors for reactions and replies to its posts. Anyone who engages gets a tailored response that references what they reacted to and presents the paid group as the natural next step. The message feels relevant rather than like a generic sales pitch.

Content generation pipeline

Each morning, the system pulls the latest watch list movements and generates a Telegram post automatically. Short, punchy, and genuinely useful — the kind of content that gives people a reason to stay in the group and a reason to want more.

Conversion tracking

Every link the bot posts includes a tracking code so we know exactly how many people clicked through to WOP and how many became paying members. This tells us the exact revenue generated by the Telegram revival in real time.

The revenue case
Why this is the fastest return on investment of any sprint

4,000 people. Conservative 1% conversion to paid membership at £99/month = £3,960 in new monthly revenue. That is the entire build cost of this sprint recovered within the first month. At 2% conversion it is £7,920 per month. These are warm contacts who already know Deb and SPA — they are not cold leads.

The member-facing app

What we build
The avatar and voice clone

Deb records 30-60 minutes of natural footage — talking normally, not scripted. This is used to train a digital avatar model using a tool called HeyGen. Separately, 10-15 minutes of clean audio is used to clone Deb's voice using ElevenLabs. The result is a digital twin that can deliver any script with Deb's face and voice.

This is a one-time setup. Once trained, the avatar produces unlimited videos. The more training footage provided, the more realistic the result.

The script generation pipeline

Three types of scripts are generated automatically each week:

Watch list commentary — what is on the list, what to watch, the signal explained simply
Spartan strategy explainers — 60-second educational breakdowns of how the strategy works
Member win highlights — converting profit screenshots from the WhatsApp group into video social proof

Scripts are written for 30, 60, and 90 second formats — matched to the platform they are going to.

Programmatic video rendering

Once scripts are approved, they are fed into HeyGen via its API — not manually uploaded one by one. The system renders all approved videos overnight in a single automated run. A week of content is produced without anyone sitting at a computer doing it.

Automated editing

Each rendered video goes through an editing pipeline automatically. Captions are generated and embedded. Deb's branding and logo are added. The video is cropped into the correct format for each platform — vertical for Instagram Reels and TikTok, horizontal for YouTube. Output is platform-ready.

Scheduling and posting

Finished videos are loaded into a scheduling tool that posts them automatically at the best times for each platform. Deb's only involvement is reviewing and approving scripts on Monday. Everything else happens without him.

The human review step — this is non-negotiable

Deb specifically said on our call that he does not want AI content to be so robotic that people can tell. We agree completely. The review step exists for exactly this reason. Scripts go to Deb or a VA for approval before any video is rendered. Nothing publishes without a human reading it first. The AI does the heavy lifting — generating, rendering, editing, scheduling. The human ensures nothing sounds wrong before it goes out. This is already how Deb's newsletters work with his ghostwriter. We apply the same model to video.
